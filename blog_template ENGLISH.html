Hello.

What happened to your English ability in the first year of studying in the United States?

It's been a year since I started studying abroad in the United States, so I'll record how much I've grown.



Before going to the US:

The deviation value is 50. I covered about 70% of the junior high school range, but I didn't study much at high school, so my English ability was "Eiken 3rd grade". I didn't even study in advance at all because I suddenly went to America.




1-3 months to America:

Study for about 15 hours a day Immediately after I went there, I took a placement test at a language school, but I could hardly solve it. Mental will also come. The children around me talked to each other and were interested. I was disappointed, but I couldn't understand 30% of what the teacher was saying, so I said "Yes" anyway according to the atmosphere. When I think about it now, I was discriminated against at this time, but I didn't know what I was saying at that time, so I listened to it very seriously. Of course I didn't think it was discrimination.




4 months to America:

Study for about 15 hours a day After about three months, God dwelled in my ears. It ’s true. About half the time, I started to understand what I was saying. Above all, I have a lot of "word power", so I can hear it more and more. As for speaking, I learned some "useful phrases", so I will call them repeatedly. It's a parrot. By this time, I had a really good relationship with a Chinese student who entered a language school at the same time because I couldn't understand the poor English.




4-7 months to America:

70% of listening is heard. It's almost no problem listening, but sometimes when I go to a fast food restaurant, my English is too early to hear and I lose my confidence. However, half of them are bad. (Perhaps). The reading speed increases considerably around here, and even essays and research papers of strange length are used. Of course, there are many words that I don't understand, but some beginners can read the books because they understand the meaning somehow. The language school you took care of here is a graduate.




8 months to the US:

I made a shift change from a language school to university classes. I am afraid that I can not understand even 30% in the first class. I'm just graduated from a language school as a professor! When I go to say something with a Chinese student, I'm gonna close my eyes on grammar. did. After all, when I entered university, I had more opportunities to speak compared to a language school, so I was impatient. I manage to talk to my American friends and keep my English. At this point, I was listening to Eiken Level 2 listening, but it sounds very late. Probably, if it is Eiken 2nd grade, it will be almost perfect.




Current: 1st year in the US:

I've returned to Japan in Corona, but now I'm doing Web related work with an American. About 20% of them still don't understand what they are saying, but in everyday life, there is absolutely no problem. I can manage to get through the presentation in the undergraduate department, even though I'm sweating through the holes.



What did you think. It was a little silly, but I feel that if I do it normally, I will be able to talk to some extent in a year.

Please let us know your growth.
